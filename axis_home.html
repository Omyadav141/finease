Axis Bank Home Loan

1. Types of Home Loans Offered by Axis Bank

  • Home Loan for Purchase: Standard loan for purchasing a new or resale home.
  • Home Loan Balance Transfer: Transfer your existing home loan from another bank to Axis Bank at potentially lower interest rates.
  • Top-up Loan: Additional funds over an existing home loan.
  • Fast Forward Home Loan: Allows borrowers to avail of EMI waivers after certain years of successful repayment.
  • Super Saver Loan: A unique option where the loan account is linked to a savings account, helping reduce interest based on savings deposited.
  • Shubh Aarambh Home Loan: Offers an EMI waiver for early repayment.

2. Key Features and Benefits

  • Loan Amount: Loans range from ₹1 lakh to ₹5 crore, depending on eligibility.
  • Interest Rate: Starts at around 8.75% per annum (may vary depending on loan type, amount, and tenure).
  • Repayment Tenure: Up to 30 years, based on applicant’s eligibility.
  • Flexible Repayment Options: EMIs, pre-closure, and part-prepayment facilities.
  • Zero Prepayment Penalty: For floating-rate loans, borrowers can prepay without any penalty.
  • Home Loan Insurance: Axis Bank offers loan insurance to cover the loan amount in case of unforeseen events.
  • Top-Up Loan Facility: Allows extra borrowing over the existing loan.

3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: Typically between 21 and 60 years (for salaried) or up to 65 years (for self-employed).
  • Income: Minimum monthly income should be at least ₹30,000 (varies based on loan type and amount).
  • Employment Status: Salaried individuals with a stable job, professionals, and self-employed individuals.
  • Credit Score: A good credit history, usually a credit score of 750 or above, enhances chances of loan approval.

4. Documents Required

  • Identity Proof: Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, or PAN card.
  • Address Proof: Utility bills, Aadhaar card, Passport, or Driving License.
  • Income Proof:
    • Salaried: Last 3 months' salary slips, Form 16, and 6 months' bank statements.
    • Self-Employed: Financial statements, ITR for the last 3 years, and bank statements.
  • Property Documents: Sale deed, property agreement, and property chain documents.
  • Other Documents: Passport-sized photos, and processing fee cheque.

5. Fees and Charges

  • Processing Fee: Usually 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount, subject to a minimum fee.
  • Legal & Technical Charges: Additional charges for legal and property valuation services.
  • Prepayment Charges: Nil for floating-rate loans; charges may apply to fixed-rate loans.
  • Loan Conversion Fees: In case of switching interest rates from fixed to floating or vice versa.
  • Stamp Duty: Based on state regulations, usually paid by the borrower.

6. Interest Rate Options

  • Floating Rate: Subject to changes with market conditions; linked to MCLR or Repo Rate.
  • Fixed Rate: Fixed for a set period (usually 1-10 years), after which it may convert to floating.

7. Application Process

  • Online Application: Available on the Axis Bank website or mobile app. Document submission can often be done digitally.
  • Branch Visit: Visit a local branch for in-person assistance.
  • Loan Approval & Disbursement: Approval based on eligibility and credit evaluation. Loan is usually disbursed within 7-10 working days after approval.

8. Special Schemes and Offers

  • EMI Waivers: Certain loans like the Shubh Aarambh offer EMI waivers for early payment.
  • Customized Interest Rates: For women borrowers and joint ownership cases.

9. Loan Repayment Options

  • EMI: Monthly EMI based on tenure and interest rate.
  • Prepayment: Available for reducing principal and interest over time.
  • Balance Transfer: Allows moving existing loan to Axis for potentially lower interest rates.

10. Axis Bank Home Loan Calculator

  • Axis Bank provides an online home loan calculator, helping you estimate EMI, interest outgo, and repayment timeline based on different loan amounts, interest rates, and tenures.
