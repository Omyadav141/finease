3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Indian citizenship is required.
  • Age: Between 18 and 35 years old.
  • Education Admission: Proof of confirmed admission to a recognized university or institution.
  • Co-applicant: A co-applicant, typically a parent or guardian, is required.

4. Eligible Courses

  • Domestic Education: Professional courses like engineering, medical, management, law, and postgraduate degrees.
  • International Education: Graduate, postgraduate, and professional courses at recognized institutions abroad.

5. Interest Rates

  • Interest Rates: Varies based on the loan amount, collateral, and creditworthiness, with fixed or floating options.
  • Discounts: Some students may qualify for discounted rates if admitted to prestigious institutions.

6. Repayment Terms

  • Repayment Tenure: Flexible repayment terms ranging from 5 to 15 years.
  • Moratorium Period: Repayment can start after completing the course or securing a job, whichever comes first.
  • Interest Payment During Moratorium: Interest may need to be serviced during the moratorium period.

7. Collateral Requirements

  • Unsecured Loans: Available for loans up to a certain limit.
  • Secured Loans: Higher loan amounts generally require collateral like property, fixed deposits, or liquid assets.

8. Processing Fees & Charges

  • Processing Fee: A nominal processing fee, typically a small percentage of the loan amount.
  • Prepayment Charges: May be waived or reduced, depending on the loan terms.

9. Documents Required

  • Application Form: Completed form with passport-sized photographs.
  • KYC Documents: Aadhaar, PAN, etc., for both the applicant and co-applicant.
  • Academic Records: Marksheets, certificates from previous institutions.
  • Proof of Admission: Admission letter from the educational institution.
  • Cost Break-up: Fee structure from the institution.
  • Income Proof of Co-applicant: Salary slips, bank statements, or IT returns.
  • Collateral Documents: Property papers or fixed deposit certificates if applicable.

10. Additional Features and Benefits

  • Tax Benefits: Interest paid may qualify for tax deductions under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act.
  • Flexible Disbursement: Loans can be disbursed in tranches based on the institution's fee schedule.
  • Online Management: Easy online access to manage the loan account, payments, and statements.
