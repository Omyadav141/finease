Axis Bank Education Loan

1. Types of Education Loans

  • Education Loan for Studies in India: For students pursuing undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional courses in India.
  • Education Loan for Studies Abroad: For students enrolled in programs like undergraduate and postgraduate studies, including professional and technical courses at recognized institutions outside India.
  • Skill Development Loans: These loans cater to students pursuing skill development or vocational training programs.

2. Loan Amount

  • Domestic Studies: Loan amounts vary depending on the course and the reputation of the institution. Generally, loans up to INR 10 lakh are available.
  • Studies Abroad: Typically, loans up to INR 20 lakh are available, with higher amounts possible for select institutions or courses.
  • Skill Development Loans: Generally available up to INR 1.5 lakh, covering short-term programs.

3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Indian citizens or NRIs.
  • Age: Between 18-35 years (age limits may vary slightly based on the loan type).
  • Co-applicant Requirement: A co-applicant is typically required (parent, guardian, or spouse).
  • Academic Record: A consistent academic record is expected.
  • Admission Requirement: Confirmed admission in recognized institutions in India or abroad before loan disbursal.

4. Loan Coverage

  • Tuition Fees: Full or partial coverage based on the institution's reputation and the student’s academic background.
  • Other Fees: Exam fees, library fees, lab fees, caution deposits, and refundable deposits supported by institution bills or receipts.
  • Travel Costs: Included for overseas studies.
  • Living Expenses: Expenses like hostel fees, study materials, laptops (if essential for the course), and other related costs.

5. Interest Rates

  • Axis Bank's education loans generally range from 9% to 13% depending on the loan type, loan amount, and course.
  • Special discounts may apply for premium institutions.
  • Interest rates are subject to the bank’s base lending rates, with female students often receiving an interest concession.

6. Repayment and Moratorium Period

  • Moratorium Period: Generally covers the course duration plus a 6-12 month grace period post-course completion.
  • Repayment Tenure: Usually between 5 to 15 years, depending on the loan amount.
  • EMI Starts: After the moratorium period. Interest during the study period can be serviced monthly, reducing the overall cost.

7. Documents Required

  • Identity Proof: PAN, Aadhaar, Passport, etc.
  • Address Proof: Recent utility bills, Aadhaar, Passport, etc.
  • Income Proof of Co-applicant: Salary slips, ITR, or bank statements.
  • Academic Documents: Marksheets, admission letter from the educational institution.
  • Cost of Study Estimate: Documents detailing fees and other expenses.
  • Passport/Visa for Overseas Education: For students applying for foreign studies.

8. Processing Fees and Charges

  • Processing Fee: Usually 1-2% of the loan amount or capped as per the bank’s policy.
  • Prepayment Charges: Typically nil if paid after the moratorium. If pre-closure is within the moratorium period, charges may apply.
  • Other Charges: Include documentation fees, late payment penalties, etc.

9. Application Process

  • Online Application: Axis Bank provides an online portal for quick application and eligibility checks.
  • Offline Application: Students can apply through Axis Bank branches.
  • Loan Sanction & Disbursement: Loan is sanctioned upon verification, with funds disbursed directly to the institution for fees, or to the borrower for other expenses.

10. Insurance Coverage

  • Axis Bank offers optional insurance coverage on education loans, providing financial protection in case of unforeseen circumstances affecting repayment capability.
