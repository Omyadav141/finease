IndusInd Bank - Indus Young Saver Account

The Indus Young Saver Account is designed to help children under 18 develop saving habits while providing parental control and guidance.

Key Features

Target Audience Children under the age of 18. The account is managed by a parent or guardian.
Interest Rates Competitive interest rates, calculated daily and credited monthly.
Parental Control Parents/Guardians can set limits on the debit card, monitor spending, and manage the account until the child turns 18.
Debit Card Optional debit card with controlled spending limits and daily transaction caps.
Online Banking Parents can manage the account via IndusNet (internet banking) and the IndusMobile app.
Educational Tools Resources to help kids learn about money management and saving.
No Minimum Balance Low or zero minimum balance requirement, depending on the branch.

Eligibility

Children below 18 years of age. The account must be opened as a joint account with a parent or guardian.

Documents Required

  • For the Child (Minor): Birth Certificate, Aadhaar Card, School ID.
  • For the Parent/Guardian: Aadhaar Card, Passport, PAN Card, Voter ID.
  • Recent passport-sized photographs of the child and the parent/guardian.

Benefits

  • Financial Literacy: Teach children the importance of saving and managing money early in life.
  • Controlled Spending: Set spending limits and monitor transactions with an optional debit card.
  • No High Balance Requirement: No pressure to maintain high balances, making it easy for children to start saving.
  • Secure and Safe: Parents have full control and receive alerts for every transaction.
  • Reward Programs: Some accounts offer rewards and benefits like discounts on shopping and entertainment.

Fees and Charges

  • Low or zero minimum balance requirement.
  • Limited free ATM withdrawals, charges apply after limits.
  • Free debit card, but premium cards may have fees.

Parental Control and Monitoring

Parents can monitor and control the account through online and mobile banking, ensuring children develop good financial habits safely.

How to Open the Account

  • Visit the nearest IndusInd Bank branch with the necessary documents.
  • Fill out the account opening form with the parent/guardian as the primary account holder.
  • The bank may issue an optional debit card for the child.

Contact and Support

For more information, parents can contact customer care via phone or email, or visit the nearest IndusInd Bank branch.
