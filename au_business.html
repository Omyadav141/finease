AU Small Finance Bank Business Loan

1. Types of Business Loans Offered

  • Working Capital Loans: Designed for day-to-day operational expenses.
  • Term Loans: For business expansion, purchasing machinery, or long-term investments.
  • Overdraft Facility: A flexible loan linked to the business account that allows withdrawals beyond the available balance.
  • Invoice/Receivables Financing: Provides immediate funds based on pending invoices.
  • Supply Chain Finance: Tailored for businesses involved in supply chains to manage receivables and payables.
  • Commercial Vehicle Loans: Loans for the purchase of commercial vehicles.
  • Construction Equipment Loans: Loans for purchasing construction equipment.

2. Key Features

  • Loan Amount: Can range from ₹50,000 to several crores, depending on the loan type and borrower profile.
  • Interest Rate: Competitive, typically based on the Prime Lending Rate (PLR) and subject to risk profile, loan amount, and tenure.
  • Loan Tenure: Flexible options; commonly ranges from 1 to 5 years, with some loans extending up to 7 years.
  • Repayment Options: Customizable to suit cash flow needs, including monthly installments, EMIs, or bullet payments.

3. Eligibility Criteria

  • Business Age: Minimum of 2-3 years in operation, with some flexibility for newer ventures under certain conditions.
  • Annual Turnover: Minimum turnover requirements based on loan type, usually ₹10-25 lakhs.
  • Profitability: A profitable track record is often preferred, but the bank may consider startups on a case-by-case basis.
  • Collateral: Some loans may be secured by collateral, such as property, equipment, or assets, with collateral-free loans available under government schemes or specific profiles.
  • Credit Score: A good credit score improves approval chances, but flexibility exists based on the business profile.

4. Documentation Required

  • Business Registration Proof: GST registration, Udyam registration, or other relevant licenses.
  • KYC Documents: PAN, Aadhar, or Passport for proprietors/partners.
  • Financial Statements: Last 2-3 years of audited financials, including Profit & Loss statements and balance sheets.
  • Bank Statements: Past 6-12 months.
  • Ownership Proof: For secured loans, ownership documents of the collateral.
  • Loan Application Form: Duly filled with business and loan details.
  • Business Plan (if required): Outline the use of funds for expansion loans.

5. Application Process

  • Online Application: AU Small Finance Bank allows for digital applications through its website or mobile app.
  • Branch Visit: For more personalized assistance, customers can visit the nearest branch.
  • Processing Time: Varies from a few days for smaller loans to up to two weeks for higher-value or secured loans.

6. Fees and Charges

  • Processing Fee: Typically 1-3% of the loan amount.
  • Prepayment Charges: Variable depending on the tenure and loan type, waived for certain government-backed loans.
  • Late Payment Penalty: Standard penalties apply for delayed payments.
  • Stamp Duty and Legal Fees: Applicable as per state law for secured loans involving collateral.

7. Additional Benefits and Facilities

  • Dedicated Relationship Manager: For high-value loans, providing end-to-end assistance.
  • Flexible Disbursal Options: Immediate fund disbursal upon approval.
  • Special Schemes for MSMEs: Includes loans under schemes like Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana, CGTMSE, etc., for lower-cost financing.
