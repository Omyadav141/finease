Explore the features and benefits of IndusInd Bank's Basic Savings Account and see how it can meet your banking needs.

IndusInd Bank Basic Savings Account

Key Features Zero Balance Account: No requirement to maintain a minimum balance.
Interest Rate: Interest is calculated on the daily closing balance and credited quarterly.
Debit Card: A complimentary RuPay or Visa debit card is issued, providing access to ATMs for cash withdrawals and shopping.
ATM Transactions: Free limited transactions at IndusInd Bank ATMs and limited free transactions at non-IndusInd Bank ATMs as per RBI guidelines.
Transaction Limits: Maximum withdrawals per month are limited; additional withdrawals may attract charges. Free services like passbook updates, cheque issuance, and deposit/withdrawal requests are offered up to a certain limit.
Internet and Mobile Banking: Free access to internet and mobile banking for fund transfers, bill payments, account management, and more.
Chequebook: Includes a chequebook, but issuance may be limited (additional chequebooks may incur fees).
Passbook: Provided for easy account tracking.
Maximum Account Balance: No minimum balance requirement, but there may be a maximum balance limit as per RBI regulations (typically ₹50,000 at any point in time).
Eligibility Individuals: Any resident Indian aged 18 years or above. Joint accounts can be opened with any eligible individual.
Documents Required Proof of Identity (KYC requirements): Aadhaar card, Passport, Voter ID, PAN card, or any government-approved ID.
Proof of Address: Aadhaar, Utility Bills (recent), Passport, Voter ID, or other valid address proof.
Passport-Sized Photographs: Recent photographs (usually 1-2).
Additional Documents: For minors or senior citizens, additional documentation like a birth certificate or senior citizen card may be required.
Fees and Charges Account Opening Charges: No charges for opening the account.
Non-maintenance Charges: No penalties for not maintaining a specific balance.
Debit Card Charges: Typically no annual fee for the debit card, but premium versions may have associated costs.
Transaction Charges: Limited free transactions at ATMs and bank branches; exceeding the limit may incur charges. Free electronic fund transfers via NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS (subject to certain limits).
Benefits Financial Inclusion: Helps individuals with low or no income to access banking services.
Convenient Banking: Provides access to essential banking services such as deposits, withdrawals, and online transactions.
Government Subsidies: Can be used to receive government subsidies, pensions, and other direct benefits.
Accessible: Available across all urban, semi-urban, and rural branches of IndusInd Bank.
Savings Encouragement: Encourages small savings and inculcates the habit of banking among individuals.
Restrictions Transaction Limits: Limitations on the number of withdrawals and other transactions (usually four withdrawals per month, including ATM and branch transactions).
Balance Cap: Cap on the maximum balance that can be held in the account (typically ₹50,000).
Credit Transaction Limits: Total credit in the account should not exceed ₹1,00,000 in a financial year, as per BSBDA guidelines.
Ideal For Individuals with low banking requirements, those looking for an account with no minimum balance requirement, and those who want to use the account for receiving government benefits and subsidies.
