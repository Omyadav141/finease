Kotak Mahindra Business Loan

1. Types of Kotak Business Loans

  • Term Loans: For long-term business expenses, such as expansion or infrastructure.
  • Working Capital Loans: Helps manage daily operational costs.
  • Machinery Loans: Financing for purchasing or upgrading machinery.
  • Commercial Vehicle Loans: For purchasing commercial vehicles.
  • Overdraft Facilities: Flexible credit line for managing cash flow.
  • Trade Loans: Includes import/export financing for international business.
  • SME Loans: Specifically targeted at small and medium enterprises.

2. Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: Applicants should generally be between 25 and 65 years of age.
  • Business Vintage: Minimum 2–3 years of business continuity.
  • Income Requirements: Proof of stable income, assessed via bank statements, tax returns, etc.
  • Credit Score: A good credit score (750+) enhances approval chances.

3. Documents Required

  • Business Proof: Registration certificates, partnership deeds, or incorporation certificate.
  • Financial Documents: Last 2-3 years' income tax returns, profit and loss statements, balance sheets.
  • Bank Statements: Typically for the last 6–12 months.
  • KYC Documents: ID proof (Aadhar, Passport), address proof, PAN card.
  • Ownership/Lease Agreement: Required if collateral is involved.

4. Loan Features

  • Loan Amount: Typically ranges from ₹2 Lakhs to several crores, depending on business type and financial health.
  • Tenure: Ranges from 12 months up to 5 years (or longer in some cases).
  • Interest Rates: Varies by product type, business profile, and tenure, typically ranging between 10–20% per annum.
  • Processing Fee: Typically between 1% and 2% of the loan amount.

5. Application Process

  • Online Application: Kotak offers online application portals for easy access.
  • Document Submission: Physical or digital document submission available.
  • Processing Time: Approval within 7–10 working days post-document verification.

6. Collateral and Security

  • Unsecured Loans: Many SME loans may not require collateral, though interest rates may be higher.
  • Secured Loans: Higher loan amounts may require collateral, such as property, equipment, or vehicles.

7. Other Features

  • Prepayment Options: Partial or full prepayment options may be available, typically after a lock-in period.
  • Loan Top-Up: Existing loan holders may apply for top-ups based on repayment history.
